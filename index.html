About Bosnia and Herzegovina

Bosnia and Herzegovina is a heart-shaped land in the Balkans, known for its deep cultural roots, natural beauty, and rich history. From Ottoman-era architecture to Austro-Hungarian elegance, the country is a stunning mix of East and West.

Things to Do

  • Walk across the historic Stari Most bridge in Mostar
  • Explore the old bazaar and mosques in Sarajevo
  • Hike in Sutjeska or Una National Parks
  • Visit medieval towns like Jajce and Travnik
  • Swim at Kravice Waterfalls

Accommodation & Food

Whether you prefer boutique hotels, mountain lodges, or cozy apartments, Bosnia and Herzegovina has a wide range of accommodation options. Local cuisine is a delicious mix of Balkan and Ottoman influences – try ćevapi, burek, and traditional Bosnian coffee.

How to Get to Bosnia and Herzegovina

You can enter Bosnia and Herzegovina via international airports in Sarajevo, Mostar, and Banja Luka. Buses and trains connect the country with neighbors like Croatia, Serbia, and Montenegro. Roads are scenic and safe for road trips.
